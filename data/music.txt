Bossa Nova: The Cool Brazilian Wave
Bossa Nova, which translates to "new trend" or "new wave," is a sophisticated style of Brazilian music that exploded out of Rio de Janeiro in the late 1950s. The structural DNA of the genre is a smooth fusion that blends traditional high-energy Brazilian Samba rhythms with the relaxed tones of American Cool Jazz. In terms of overall vibe, Bossa Nova stands in stark contrast to loud carnival samba by remaining relaxed, intimate, and almost whispered, relying heavily on nylon-string acoustic guitars, swaying rhythms, and complex, jazzy chord structures. The definitive icon of this musical movement is the classic song "The Girl from Ipanema," which serves as the ultimate introduction to its timeless sound.
