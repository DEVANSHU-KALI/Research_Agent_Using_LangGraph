Hypertrophy: Building Bigger Engines
In simple terms, hypertrophy is the physical enlargement of an organ or tissue caused entirely by an increase in the size of its existing cells, rather than growing an entirely higher number of cells. In a fitness context, when you lift heavy weights, you aren't actually growing brand-new muscles from scratch, but rather making your existing muscle fibers thicker and stronger by repairing the microscopic tears caused by working out. This cellular growth trigger happens when tissues are subjected to heavy mechanical tension, metabolic stress, or direct damage, forcing them to adapt so they can handle that exact load easier next time. This process stands in sharp contrast to hyperplasia, which specifically refers to a condition where your body creates a higher quantity of cells altogether.
